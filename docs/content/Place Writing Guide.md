# TOURISK PLACE WRITING GUIDE

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

Tourisk does not describe places.

Tourisk creates the desire to discover them.

Every place should make the player want to take a few more steps.

---

# Our Goal

When a player opens a place, they should feel:

"I want to go there."

Not:

"I've read Wikipedia."

---

# Writing Style

Simple.

Warm.

Inspiring.

Emotional.

Human.

Never academic.

---

# Maximum Length

50–120 words.

People are walking.

Not reading articles.

---

# The Golden Rule

Never explain everything.

Always leave something to discover.

Curiosity is stronger than information.

---

# What We Never Write

❌ Dry history.

❌ Long dates.

❌ Lists of architects.

❌ Encyclopedic language.

❌ Tourist brochure clichés.

---

# What We Always Write

✅ Why should I walk here?

✅ What emotion does this place create?

✅ When is the best moment to visit?

✅ What makes this place special?

---

# Structure

## Title

Simple.

Example:

Cascade

---

## One Emotional Sentence

Capture attention immediately.

Example:

One of the most beautiful places to watch the sunset over Yerevan.

---

## Main Description

Tell a short story.

Not facts.

Create curiosity.

---

## Local Secret

Whenever possible, include something locals know.

Example:

Many locals come here after sunset simply to enjoy the city lights.

---

## Finish With Inspiration

End every place with a reason to continue exploring.

Example:

Maybe your next favorite place is just around the next corner.

---

# Tone of Voice

Friendly.

Curious.

Respectful.

Never arrogant.

Never overly excited.

Never fake.

---

# Tourisk Language

Use words like:

discover

explore

walk

journey

story

hidden

view

memory

adventure

world

Never use words like:

must

required

forced

mission

objective

task

---

# One Place = One Memory

Every place should become part of the player's story.

Not just another point on the map.

---

# Final Question

Before publishing any place ask:

"Would this description make me walk there?"

If the answer is no,

rewrite it.
